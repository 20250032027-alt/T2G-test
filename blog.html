T2G Breathes New Life into the Coconut Industry in Misamis Oriental

April 3, 2024

T2G started in May 2017 with a community-based goal: do something meaningful with a resource that Misamis Oriental had in abundance but wasn't fully using. Coconut trees were everywhere in San Isidro, Jasaan. What was coming out of them was mostly tuba and copra. Gemma Dela Vega-Emata, T2G's Managing Director, saw that gap and built something in it.

The name is straightforward. Coconut trunk, turned into something of real value. Gemma and her co-founders, mostly development workers from the cooperative sector, knew the global demand for coco sugar was already high. The Philippine market wasn't there yet, but they weren't building only for the Philippine market.

Seven years in, the facility in San Isidro has gone from a wok on a stove to a mechanized processing line. FDA compliance is now a reality. They print their own pouches. "Sa sector ng coconut, kami ang maganda 'yung facility," Gemma says -- in the coconut sector, theirs stands out.

The product range has grown too. Coco Rush, the original coconut sugar brand, is still the core product. T2G has since developed Coco Savor, a seasoning sauce from coconut sap created with DOST-ITDI. Low sodium, gluten-free, allergen-free, already at commercial stage.

The next goals: global market entry, organic certification, and getting products in front of everyday consumers -- not just health food buyers. "Para mabigyan ng chance 'yung product natin sa pangkalahatan na consumers," Gemma says. For everyone who decides they want to eat better, not just those who already can afford to.

Why Coconut Tappers in Balingasag Earn More Working with T2G

April 3, 2024

Tatay Henry Yecyec from Barangay Linabo in Balingasag found his way to T2G through a friend's recommendation. He owns a lot of coconut land. Before T2G, most of it was tied to copra: harvest infrequently, get paid quarterly, repeat.

Now he delivers five containers of coconut sap every Wednesday. Each container is worth PHP 1,750. That is a weekly income cycle, not a quarterly one. For his tappers, Tatay Henry says conditions improved noticeably -- "hayahay na ang sweldo kay kada semana naman," the wages come regularly now rather than appearing once every few months.

Jimrex Cinco is a T2G coconut tapper and member of PAGSAKA, T2G's partner cooperative. He used to work construction in a different city, away from his family. His daily wage there was PHP 300. At T2G, he earns PHP 4,000 to PHP 5,000 a week. He climbs the trees four times a day, boils the sap into syrup in a wok, loads it into containers for delivery. His dream is simple: send his children through school. The cooperative getting bigger means more tappers get that chance.

For context, a coconut farmer supplying sap to T2G can earn around PHP 40,000 in a month. Copra from the same trees cannot come close to that. T2G currently works with 25 to 30 tappers from Balingasag, Claveria, and Jasaan. The reason they cannot take on more: working capital. There are tappers without buyers. That is the gap T2G is trying to close.

Coco Savor: T2G's New Seasoning Sauce is Headed to Market

April 3, 2024

Most of T2G's products come from coconut sap. Coco Savor, T2G's seasoning sauce, is no different -- but it took a longer road to get here.

T2G developed it with DOST-ITDI, the government's food technology research body. Involving DOST-ITDI meant the product went through real formulation and testing work. The result is a coconut sap-based seasoning sauce that is low in sodium, gluten-free, and allergen-free.

Soy sauce is the default in Filipino cooking. It is not any of those things. People on restricted diets mostly just use less of it. Coco Savor is built to be an actual replacement, not a compromise.

Gemma Dela Vega-Emata, T2G's Managing Director, says the product is already at commercial stage with a dedicated processing facility in the works. The goal is a broad market, not a health food niche. "Para mabigyan ng chance 'yung product natin sa pangkalahatan na consumers," she says -- for everyone choosing to eat healthily, not just those who already shop at premium grocers. Organic certification is also on the roadmap, aimed at export readiness.

Bake Healthier with T2G Coco Sugar: Chocolate Chip Cookies

April 3, 2024

Coco sugar swaps 1-for-1 with white sugar in most recipes. Same dissolution, same binding, same behavior in a hot oven. It adds a faint caramel warmth that white sugar doesn't have. In chocolate chip cookies you barely notice it as a difference -- the cookies just taste a bit more interesting. Use dark chocolate chips and you'll notice it more. Use milk chocolate and you won't notice at all, which is kind of the point.

T2G's coco sugar has a glycemic index of 35. Refined white sugar sits around 65. For anyone managing blood sugar, that gap is worth paying attention to.

Basic recipe (makes about 24 cookies):
200g T2G Coco Sugar, 225g unsalted butter (softened), 2 eggs, 1 tsp vanilla extract, 300g all-purpose flour, 1 tsp baking soda, 1/2 tsp salt, 200g chocolate chips.

Cream the butter and coco sugar until pale. Add the eggs one at a time, then the vanilla. Stir in the flour, baking soda, and salt until just combined. Fold in the chocolate chips. Scoop onto a lined baking sheet and bake at 180C for 10 to 12 minutes. Pull them out when they look slightly underdone. Let them cool on the pan -- they firm up as they cool. That is the whole recipe.
